**RISC-V China Summit Steering Committee Charter**

*History, committees and rules*

Summary

This document aims to explain the origins, committee structure, rules, and guidelines of the RISC-V China Summit. Since the inaugural China Summit in 2021, the steering committee has accumulated relatively rich experience. This document aims to provide a more coherent and systematic framework for future summits.

# History

The RISC-V China Summit (RVSC) began in 2021, organized and initiated by Guo Xiongfei and Wu Wei, then China liaisons of the RISC-V International Foundation (RVI), with the support of then-CEO of RVI (Calista Redmond). ShanghaiTech University served as the host of the first RISC-V China Summit, and the Institute of Software, Chinese Academy of Sciences, served as a co-host. From 2022 to 2025, the second to fifth RISC-V China Summits were held online and in Beijing, Hangzhou, and Shanghai, respectively.

The initial RVSC Steering Committee (SC) was honorary, with Wu Wei and Guo Xiongfei handling the actual organization. As the influence of the RISC-V China Summit grew, the decision-making process regarding the selection of the city and host city attracted industry attention, necessitating a more professional decision-making process to ensure the neutrality, inclusivity, and universality of the RISC-V China Summit. In 2024, the RVI Board of Directors (BoD) decided to form a new SC with Guo Xiongfei and Wu Wei through voluntary application by BoD members. A total of seven RVI BoD members or designated representatives joined (see Appendix 1 for the list). After the reorganized SC was established, a simple majority vote was used to select the city and host city for the 2025 RISC-V China Summit, but discussions were not held regarding the SC's own membership additions or other bylaws. Between 2024 and 2025, the SC received numerous applications to join; in August 2025, the SC began discussing and formulating its own bylaws.

# Mission and Positioning

The mission of the RISC-V China Summit (RVSC) Steering Committee (SC) is to serve as the guiding and oversight body for the RISC-V China Summit, to coordinate with RVI on the organization and promotion of the RVSC, to ensure the proper use of the "RISC-V" and "RISC-V Summit" brands, and to promote the development of RISC-V in China and globally.

The SC is responsible for developing various processes related to RVSC and overseeing their implementation. The SC votes to determine the host city, organizer, and co-organizer of the RISC-V China Summit each year, appoints key roles such as the Summit Chair, Program Committee Chair, and Organizing Committee Chair, and oversees the entire organization of the RISC-V China Summit to ensure the summit's financial balance, transparency, and global fairness in sponsorship opportunities.

# The joining and leaving of committee members

9. New committee candidates must be jointly nominated by two committee members, discussed at the SC meeting, and a qualification vote must be initiated. A candidate must receive more than half of the votes from all committee members to be admitted. The new committee member's status takes effect the day after the vote.
10. Members may apply to step down during their term. Applications to step down do not require a vote but must be announced at the SC meeting and included in the meeting minutes. The outgoing member's status expires the day after the SC meeting concludes.
11. A member can be removed from office by a joint proposal from two members. The removal requires discussion at an SC meeting and a vote. The removal takes effect if more than half of all members vote in favor, and the removed member is disqualified on the day the vote is completed.
12. **Committee members are obligated to attend SC meetings and participate in voting. Membership will be automatically terminated if a member is absent from two consecutive SC meetings without a valid reason. Membership will also be automatically terminated if a member is absent from voting consecutively. Terminated members will no longer be counted in the total SC vote count. Re-enlistment of terminated members requires following the nomination and voting process for new committee members.**

# Chair and Vice Chair of the Steering Committee

13. The SC will have one Chairperson (SCC). The SCC is responsible for communicating with RVI to ensure the proper authorization of the use of the RISC-V trademark and the RISC-V Summit brand; convening SC meetings and organizing the voting process; and communicating and coordinating with all parties involved in RVSC.
14. **Candidates for the SCC must be selected from the SC committee and must have served as an SC committee member for at least two years.**SCC candidates are nominated by two committee members, discussed at the SC meeting, and then a vote is initiated on their appointment. An appointment takes effect on the day it receives more than half of the votes from all committee members. If there are multiple candidates in a competitive election, a two-round majority voting process is used: In the first round, if no candidate receives more than half the votes, the top two candidates are selected by simple majority (the one with the most votes) to advance to the second round; in the second round, one SCC is elected by two candidates with a competitive election, and the candidate with the most votes is elected.
15. Two committee members can jointly initiate a recall vote against an SCC. The joint signatories can organize an extraordinary SC meeting and initiate a vote to remove the SCC. If the number of votes in favor reaches half of the total number of SC committee members, the recall takes effect, and the SCC's status is revoked on the same day (its committee membership remains unaffected).
16. An SCC may apply to step down during their term. The application to step down must be discussed at an SC meeting and recorded in the meeting minutes, and a vote must be organized to elect a new SCC.
17. The SC has one Vice President. The Vice President serves as a substitute for the SCC, and will assume the duties of the SCC when the SCC is unable to perform its responsibilities. The appointment, removal, and resignation procedures for the Vice President are the same as those for the SCC.
18. The chairman and vice-chairman of the SC must be individuals or corporate members of the RVI.

# Meetings and Voting

19. The SC should hold at least one plenary meeting annually to discuss matters such as the admission and withdrawal of committee members, and the nomination and removal of the chairperson. Meeting notices should be sent to all committee members via email at least two weeks in advance.
20. The SCC is responsible for convening SC meetings. The Vice-Chairperson may convene such meetings if the SCC fails to perform its duties.
21. If more than half of the total number of committee members attend the SC meeting, a vote can be taken during the meeting; otherwise, voting must be done via email or the OpaVote platform.
22. The SC uses the OpaVote voting platform, which is also used by RVI, for vote counting. The SCC initiates the voting process; if the SCC has a potential conflict of interest, it can delegate the setup to an RVI employee. For platforms other than OpaVote, in-meeting voting or email voting is required.

# Amendment of the Articles of Association

23. The establishment of the initial version of this charter and subsequent revisions shall take effect only if approved by a two-thirds majority vote of all SC members.
24. Two or more SC members may propose amendments to the charter.

# RISC-V China Summit

25. SC is responsible for the planning, preparation, organization and supervision of the annual RISC-V China Summit.
26. SC is responsible for communicating with RVI regarding the timing of the RISC-V China Summit; RVI coordinates the timing of the summits in China, Europe, and North America.
27. SC determines the date of the next RISC-V China Summit at least six months in advance and publicly solicits bids for host cities and organizers; within four weeks of issuing the public solicitation notice, a vote is held to determine the host city and organizers.
28. After the organizer was determined, SC negotiated with the organizer to form a team.The summit executive team (ET) is primarily composed of the summit chairperson.It consists of a Program Committee (PC) and an Organizing Committee (OC).
29. The PC is responsible for reviewing submissions such as speeches and posters, and participating in the determination of the summit's speaking agenda. The Call for Proposals (CFP) must be open for submissions at least 16 weeks prior to the summit, and the results of the acceptance process must be announced at least 8 weeks before the summit.
30. OC is responsible for the specific preparations of the summit, including event application, venue setup, media promotion, sponsorship solicitation, and booth arrangement.
31. The SC reviews the reasonableness and fairness of the summit's sponsorship and booth proposals, and oversees the OC's recruitment of sponsors on a first-come, first-served (FIFS) basis, adhering to global fairness principles. Sponsorship proposals are proposed by the Summit Organizing Committee (OC) and must be approved by the SC before being published.
32. SC is responsible for maintaining the official website of the RISC-V China Summit and authorizes the organizers, PC, and OC to update the information as necessary.
33. The summit agenda (including the opening ceremony, main forum, sub-forums, members' days, etc.) needs to be reviewed and approved by the SC before the PC and OC are authorized to publish it on the official summit website.
34. The SC organizes a Summit Preparation Meeting (SPM) every two weeks. Attendees include all SC members, representatives of the organizers, the Summit Chair, the PC Chair, the OC Chair, and organizers closely involved in the summit preparation. The SC Chair makes decisions and authorizes various non-critical matters during the summit preparation period; for matters deemed significant by SC members or the Summit Chair, the SC discusses and votes on them.
35. The RISC-V China Summit is a non-profit event. Its funding sources mainly include financial support from the organizer, sponsorship fees, and booth fees. The revenue is used to pay for the summit venue, materials, and event service fees.
36. When major disputes or conflicts arise during the preparation process, the SC can proactively intervene and serve as the final decision-making mechanism.

## Duties and obligations of the summit General Chair, Vice chair(s), and co-chair(s)

37. The RVSC's Summit Chair (GC), Vice Chairs, and Co-Chairs are appointed by the SC.
38. GC represents the summit organizers in promoting the event, inviting sponsors, and inviting guests.
39. GC represents the summit organizers in connecting with local government and other management agencies.
40. GC, representing the summit organizers, delivered a speech at the opening ceremony.
41. GC representatives gave a media interview on behalf of the summit organizers.
42. GC is required to attend SPM on time and report to SC on the progress, problems, and risks in the summit preparation process.
43. Depending on the specific circumstances during the preparation process, the SC may appoint 1-2 vice chairpersons or co-chairpersons for the summit. The responsibilities and powers of the vice chairpersons and co-chairpersons shall not exceed those of the GC.
44. In addition to the above authorizations, any other authorizations required by the GC during the summit preparation period must be discussed by the SPM and authorized by the SC Chairman.
